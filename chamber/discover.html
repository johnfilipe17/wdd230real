Vila Velha is a Brazilian municipality located on the coast of the state of Espírito Santo, in the Southeast Region of the country. It belongs to the Metropolitan Region of Vitória and occupies an area of ​​209,965 km², of which 54.57 km² are in the urban perimeter, and the population in 2021 was estimated by the IBGE at 508,655 inhabitants, which makes the municipality the second most populous in Espírito Santo Santo, behind only Serra. The seat has an average annual temperature of 24.7 °C and the original vegetation of the municipality is dominated by the Atlantic Forest, currently having some stretches of restinga. With 99.5% of its inhabitants living in urban areas, in 2009 the city had 163 health facilities. Its Human Development Index is 0.8, which is considered very high in relation to the Brazilian average, being the second highest in the entire state. The municipality was founded on May 23, 1535 by the Portuguese Vasco Fernandes Coutinho, grantee of the Captaincy of Espírito Santo, and was its headquarters until 1549, when the capital was transferred to Vitória. It is then the oldest city in the state, with several historic buildings, such as the Church of Nossa Senhora do Rosário, the Fort of São Francisco Xavier de Piratininga, the Lighthouse of Santa Luzia and the Convent of Penha, the latter being one of one of the main tourist attractions in Espírito Santo, built between the 16th and 17th centuries and listed as a cultural heritage site by the Instituto do Patrimônio Histórico e Artístico Nacional in 1943.
